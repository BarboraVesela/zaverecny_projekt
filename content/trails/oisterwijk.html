Oisterwijkse Bossen en Vennen

Forests, dozens of fens and beautiful purple heather

A beautiful natural area east of Tilburg, Oisterwijkse Bossen en Vennen offers several walking and cycling routes though the local peatlands, fens and forests.

The so-called '14 Vennen Route' starts at the visitor centre Groot Speijck. It houses a small shop and a cafe which makes for a convenient spot to refresh right at the start, especially if you have travelled to Oisterwijk from afar. From there the route heads towards Diaconieven, the first of the fens which is usually full of water birds. Originally, this fen was owned by the ministry of a deaconess of Tilburg and was used for its nuns as a recreational area for swimming. In 1913 it was bought by Natuurmonumenten, a Dutch nature conservation organisation, which bought additional land in the area now known as the Oisterwijkse Bossen en Vennen.

***

The trail then continues past the Brandven which in the past was used to pump water whenever there was a fire in the area. The trail then continues west towards Kolkven, the largest fen in the area. Together with the other fens it was formed just after the ice age. You might meet members of the local fishing club who are allowed to take their small fishing boats out on the water. It is also one of my favourite spots to stop and enjoy the views.

From here you walk around the lake and reach a small group of fens called the Lammervennen. In the past the lambs were washed here so that their wool was cleaner and could be sold at a higher price. Heading back north you reach the Boshuis Venkraai which was originally a forest ranger residence and nowadays serves as a cafe for tourists. The next fen you reach is the Voorste Goorven from where peat was dug out.

The path which follows the lake shore contains stones and bricks from the ruins left after the bombing of Rotterdam. The Germans did so to reach their ammunition depot hidden in the forest. The path richest the northern shore of the lake and continues towards Witven from where it turns east towards Klein Aderven and Groot Aderven. Staalbergven, the next fen on the route, is the only one where swimming is allowed. By now you have hiked half of the trail length and are heading back south west past the Van Esschenven named after a famous local family.

On the other edge of the lake you can take a short detour to reach the Van Tienhoven bench. About 100 years old, it commemorates the purchase of the first piece of land in the area by Natuurmonumenten led by, among others, Pieter Van Tiehoven. He played a crucial role in protecting this but also other natural areas in The Netherlands. The route then passes two smaller fens before reaching the visitor centre again.
